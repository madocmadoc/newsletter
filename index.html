Our mission is simple.

To be catalysts for change and boldly create a world where our people and clients have equal opportunities to create, build and grow – strengthened by the things that make us different.

Huge believes that belonging and community are fostered through equality, inclusion and accessibility, and we’re at our best when all of our people are engaged and contributing to a more diverse and inclusive workplace.

This is the first Huge Diversity & Inclusion newsletter; a celebration of the work we do across the network to ensure that our employees and our communities feel celebrated and supported.

 
 
 

Diversity.

The state of possessing and maintaining a collective of diverse backgrounds, experiences, and viewpoints.

 

Inclusion

The act of ensuring that every member of the collective is positioned to use their viewpoints in service of the greater good of all.

 
 
 

Pride is here. The fight goes on.

Fifty years ago the Stonewall Riots launched the modern LGBTQ+ rights movement. Throughout the month of June (and beyond) we will be celebrating the LGBTQ+ community and the activists who paved the way. Interested in learning more about being an ally? Check this out, hang with the LGBTQ+ affinity group, and engage with some of the stuff below!

 

LGBTQ+ heroes.

Many offices will be displaying imagery illustrating the people (and symbology) at the forefront of the LGBTQ+ rights movement. 

Cards for diversity.

Huge Medellin created cards to educate people on terms specific to the LGBTQ+ community.

 

Work's a Drag.

Drag Queen Gloria Swansong gave a brief history of drag, from caveman times to today, to kick off pride in Brooklyn. Medellin & Bogota are hosting drag queens this month as well, with a focus on music and the drag experience today.

Just getting started.

Members of the LGBTQ+ affinity group (Slack: huge_lgbtq) have been working hard since April to bring panels, barbecues, movie nights, and more throughout the rest of the month.

 
 

The Black Effect: ATL.

 

RBG co-lead Apryl Gordy led a panel discussion around what it means to be a person of color in Atlanta, throughout their respective lives and diverse career paths.

The Black Effect is an ongoing series in celebration of melanin and discussion on navigating corporate settings created by affinity group RBG (Slack: affinity_rbg).

 
 

Immigration law.

On May 30th, our Immigrants at Huge affinity group hosted a Q&A discussion with our immigration lawyers to offer insights and education in these tumultuous times.

Lunches get cultured.

DC had their first (of many) themed lunches where they will experience a different culture each month through their cuisine. The first was on May 17th, Norwegian Constitution Day. 

 

Women in tech took Toronto.

Huge hosted a chat featuring five standout women in the tech & innovation industry where they discussed what it takes to succeed in the Toronto market. Our very own Hooha, the world’s first smart tampon dispenser, was also showcased.

$35,352.70.

That's the combined total spend for RBG's first Black Business Challenge. North American offices competed to see which could spend the most at black-owned businesses throughout the month of May. 3rd place: Atlanta, 2nd place: DC, 1st place: Detroit. 

 

Mental health. 

Huge LDN spent UK's Mental Health Awareness week (May 13-19) to educate the office on all the ways in which they support good mental health including Headspace memberships and their Employee Assistance Program.

The Privilege Game.

In addition to a kick-off discussion, RBG visited Huge DC to lead the office in an on-their-feet exercise to recognize how power and privilege can affect our lives even when we are not aware it's happening. Next stop: Toronto on June 26th-27th!

 
 

STOKED's not-so-secret Sauce.

 

Each year Huge partners with STOKED to empower teens (and bridge the opportunity gap) by introducing them to the principles of design thinking.

On May 30th, STOKED had their End of Year Brand Showcase, where six schools presented the brands they’d built throughout the school year. All of the brands were phenomenal, but the evening's winner was SAUCE, a card game with the intent to improve the relationships between teacher and student. The students will spend a week at Huge this summer to further build their brand.
